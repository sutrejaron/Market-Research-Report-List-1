<p><p>Tunneling Magnetoresistance (TMR) current sensors are categorized into two main market types: digital output and analog output. Digital output sensors convert the detected magnetic field variations into discrete signals, offering high precision and noise immunity suitable for digital systems. In contrast, analog output sensors provide continuous voltage or current signals proportional to the magnetic field strength, allowing for real-time monitoring and easy integration with analog systems. Both types play vital roles in applications such as automotive, industrial, and consumer electronics.</p></p>
