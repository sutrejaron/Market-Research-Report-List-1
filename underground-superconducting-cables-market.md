NbTi and NbSn are low-temperature superconductors, ideal for applications requiring cooling to near absolute zero, offering high current-carrying capacity. In contrast, Bi-2223 and YBCO represent high-temperature superconductors, which operate at higher temperatures, reducing cooling costs and enhancing efficiency. Each type serves specific needs in power transmission, contributing to improved energy efficiency, reduced losses, and increased grid reliability in smart grid technologies.</p></p>
